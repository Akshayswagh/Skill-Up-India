B.Sc. Degree in Interior Design

Why Choose a Degree in Interior Design?

Interior design is a dynamic, multidisciplinary field that integrates aesthetics, functionality, sustainability, and technology to create harmonious and efficient spaces. A B.Sc. in Interior Design equips students with technical skills, design thinking, and hands-on experience to meet the demands of residential, commercial, and institutional interior spaces.

Aligned with NEP 2020, this program incorporates experiential learning, industry exposure, digital design tools, and sustainability to prepare students for global career opportunities in architecture, interior design, and space planning.

What Will You Learn in the Program?

  • Core Principles of Interior Design & Space Planning
  • Architectural Drawing, 3D Visualization & CAD Tools
  • Furniture Design, Materials & Sustainable Practices
  • Lighting, Acoustics & Smart Home Integration
  • Project Management, Budgeting & Client Communication
  • Hands-on Industry Projects, Internships & Portfolio Development

Curriculum Overview

The B.Sc. in Interior Design is a three-year undergraduate program with a choice-based credit system (CBCS) as per NEP 2020.

  • Total Credits per Semester: 20
  • Subjects per Semester: 5 (100 marks each)
  • Project/Internship per Semester: 1 (200 marks)

Each semester focuses on core subjects, technical skills, practical training, and industry exposure to ensure well-rounded learning.
