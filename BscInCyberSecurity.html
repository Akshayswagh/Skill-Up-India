B.Sc. Degree in Cyber Security

Why Choose a Degree in Cyber Security?

In an increasingly digital world, cyber threats, data breaches, and hacking incidents are rising. Organizations worldwide are investing in cybersecurity professionals to protect their systems, networks, and sensitive information. A B.Sc. in Cyber Security prepares students to detect, analyze, and mitigate cyber threats, ensuring data privacy and security across industries such as finance, healthcare, defense, e-commerce, and government sectors.

This NEP 2020-aligned program combines multidisciplinary learning, hands-on training, and real-world cybersecurity challenges to equip students with technical expertise and industry-recognized certifications.

What Will You Learn in the Program?

  • Core Principles of Cyber Security & Ethical Hacking
  • Network Security, Cryptography & Secure Software Development
  • Cloud Security, Digital Forensics & Cyber Law
  • Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning in Cyber Security
  • Incident Response, Risk Management & Penetration Testing
  • Industry Certifications, Hands-on Labs & Capstone Projects

Curriculum Overview

The B.Sc. in Cyber Security follows the Choice-Based Credit System (CBCS) under NEP 2020, ensuring a practical, application-oriented approach.

  • Total Credits per Semester: 20
  • Subjects per Semester: 5 (100 marks each)
  • Project/Internship per Semester: 1 (200 marks)

Each semester integrates core cybersecurity subjects, programming skills, ethical hacking, cloud security, AI-driven security solutions, hands-on labs, and internships to develop industry-ready professionals.
